UP1 / UP4 PSD & viscosity data review

Nine run conditions from visc.xlsx — rotational-rheometer flow curves plus paired post-UP1 wet (m) and post-UP4 dry (p) particle-size distributions. This review checks the data and compares it against the mixer model's aggregation and viscosity closures.

1 · Aggregation onset — wet d50 vs tip speed

Below ~6.8 m/s the colloid never aggregates and stays at its 0.18 µm primary bead — cond2, the atomizer-control regime, where UP4 sets the product size. Above it (UP1-control), every condition jumps to a flat ~10 µm aggregate plateau that barely moves with tip speed and never rolls back down. The old model's milling onset would have shrunk d50 right at the highest point (cond11); the recalibration removes that.

2 · Flow curves — apparent viscosity vs shear rate (log–log)

The feed is water-thin and nearly Newtonian (n≈0.9). Mixing turns it into a strongly shear-thinning floc suspension (n≈0.44). At matched ~20% solids the post-UP1 curves fan across a 38× viscosity range — cond8 is the high outlier the model was previously anchored to; cond12 the low one.

3 · Low-shear viscosity calibration

Measured η at 12.7 s⁻¹ per condition (log scale) against the model's viscosity target. The prior closure sat at the top of the cloud; the recalibration targets the geometric mean.

4 · Two control regimes: wet → dry

Atomizer-control (cond2): no UP1 aggregate, so the atomizer sets a droplet-shell — 108× inflation (0.18→19.9 µm). UP1-control (rest): the aggregate templates the particle, dry/wet ≈ 1.2, and dry d50 falls as UP1 shear rises.

cond2 is the dispersed outlier; the eight aggregated feeds sit at wet 10.2 µm, dry 12.3 µm mean.
